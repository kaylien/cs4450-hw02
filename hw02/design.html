User-First Design

- As a normal user, I can make posts that my followers can see
* To make a post, I would go to the home page of the website and click a button that says, "Tweet" or "Make new post". It will lead me to another web page that allows me to type into a text box what I want to publish. I will then click the "Post" button on the page that will put the post online, appearing in all of the feeds of my followers and on my profile when viewed.
- As a normal user, I can follow other accounts
* To follow other accounts, I go to the home page and click the search bar, then type in the username or name of the person I would like to follow. Then a list of user profile descriptions (showing name and username) is displayed that contain the entered criteria in the search bar. If I click a profile description, I will go to that user profile. I can click the follow button to follow that new user. Now when I am looking at my feed of posts of other users, I can see the new user's posts that I just followed.
- As a normal user, I can read posts of people I follow
* I would go to the home page of the website. This will automatically have a feed of the latest 15 posts of the users that I follow in chronological order. I can click a button that chooses to load more posts following the initial home page's posts.
- As a normal user, I can delete my account
* To delete my account, I would click the link to my profile page on the home page of the website. Then I would click the settings button (that is only visible on my own profile page to myself, I cannot see anyone else's setting buttons nor can they see mine). There is a button that says "Delete account" on the page and once I click it, it will delete my account, erasing my profile page, all of my posts and "likes" on other posts, and no other users will follow me/I will not be following any other users. - As a normal user, I can "like" other posts
* To like posts, I would see them in one of two ways. Either the post is appearing on my feed in the home page, or I made a search for a user profile in the search bar on the home page and clicked on a profile that displayed all of that user's posts. Either way, once I see the post, there will be a small heart in the corner of each post and when I click it, it will add a like to that post. The post will display a count of how many likes it has received. Each post can only be liked by an individual user once.

Data-First Design

- Users
* Email address
* Username
* Posts
* Followers
* Following

- Posts
* Reference to user
* Text content of the post
* Date
* Like count
RESTful Routes:
Users can "create" more posts by clicking the make a post button on the home page, then being directed to a new page and clicking post after entering their text content
You can "retrieve" the posts you create by going onto your own profile or seeing it appear in the feed on the home page
You can "update" your post by clicking edit on it
You can "delete" your post by clicking delete on it
The index of the posts is seen on the home page as a feed of the users that you follow including your own posts
You can fill out the form for new posts and click post
Constraints: You can't delete or edit posts that aren't your own. You can't see all posts of all users indexed on the home page, only the ones you follow.
- Followers
* Reference to user
* Users being followed

RESTful Routes:
Users can "create" more users they follow by following other users
You can "retrieve" the users you follow by clicking the number of "following" on your profile page
You can "update" the users you follow by clicking follow on the profile of the user you are following, and it will lead you to a page asking if you want to follow this person, if you click "yes" it will update
You can "delete" users you follow from your follow list by reclicking the follow button on their profile (which will display unfollow if you are already following them)
The index of the users you follow is seen by clicking the number of "following" on your profile page
You can have new followers by clicking follow on their profile
Constraints: You can't follow yourself
